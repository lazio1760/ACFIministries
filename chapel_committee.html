Chapel Committee / Ushers

The following are guidelines provided to instruct the group on how to discharge its functions and duties within the local church; how to discover through worship, study and service, the will of God for themselves, for the church, and for all humanity. It provides directions on how to coordinate the total programs of the group, and help members to take active participation in the life of the church and mission of this Ministry.

Ushering is a lot more than showing or leading people to their seats in the church. Ushering is a ministry that meets the spiritual and physical needs of the church. When you usher at any of ACFI Churches, you are the first person a visitor meets when coming in the door. Your greeting of friendship and spirit of helpfulness conveys that very important first impression. Members and friends of the Church also look forward to your welcome. Whatever you can do to make people feel welcome will support their receptivity to the service of worship. Think of your role as that of a host or hostess welcoming a guest into your home. Time for you to arrive at the church is very important in this situation. You would need to be at the church at least thrity minutes before the service begins, even the Sunday school. Ushers' responsibilities include the following:

1.) To ensure that the people are humbly conducted into the Chapel/Church Hall.

2.) To give to each person as they enter the Church, the program's Leaflet, Sunday school materials, Offering envelopes, and any other information they may need for the worship.

3.) To attend to the congregation in the course of Church service and other programmes and to maintain order in services and other meetings.

4.) To take and record Sunday service attendance.

5.) To make arrangement for offering bowls and to collect the offering.

6.) To be responsible for the personal safety of the pastoral staff.

7.) To assist with prayer lines and altar Ministry (keep cover up clothes).

8.) To assist nursing mothers with their children during services.

9.) To make available on request, Membership Formsâ€™ and every other Forms that serves the Congregation, and/or direct Congregation to the Ministry's website for more information about the church.

10.) To direct people to move down the aisle to the Offering bowl/Communion table and to stand by the first pew until the members finish giving/receiving. The Ushers give/receive last.

11.) To ensure that all the Church materials and properties are properly kept in their respective places after the services and programmes.

12.) To keep the Church premises tidy and orderly before and after each program.

13.) To commit themselves to prayer and sharing the word of God regularly.
